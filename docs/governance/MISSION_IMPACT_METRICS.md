## WHY THIS MATTERS

**Most startups track:** Revenue, growth, users, engagement  
**We also track:** Lives changed, dignity restored, recovery funded, cooperation demonstrated

**Revenue is a MEANS, not the END.**

The end: AI-human cooperation that funds recovery and restores dignity.

**If we're profitable but not serving the mission, we've failed.**

### ğŸ“Š CATEGORY 1: DIRECT IMPACT (Recovery & Dignity)

**Metric 1.1: Recovery Services Funded**

**What we measure:**
- Total dollars allocated to recovery services (per Financial Covenant)
- Number of recovery programs/organizations funded
- Number of individuals served through recovery funding
- Types of services funded (housing, treatment, support groups, etc.)

**Target Allocation (from Financial Covenant):**
- Phase 1 ($0-10K MRR): $0 (building sustainability first)
- Phase 2 ($10K-50K MRR): 30% of revenue
- Phase 3 ($50K+ MRR): 50% of revenue

**How we track:**
- Monthly: Calculate allocation per covenant
- Quarterly: Distribute to recovery organizations
- Annually: Impact report on lives touched

**Success looks like:**
- By Dec 2026: $50K+ allocated to recovery services
- By Dec 2027: $500K+ allocated
- By 2028: $2M+ allocated (if we hit $400K MRR)

---

**Metric 1.2: Workers Earning Dignified Income**

**What we measure:**
- Number of unique human workers who received tasks through HumanAIOS
- Average earnings per worker per month
- Number of workers earning >$500/month (meaningful supplemental income)
- Number of workers earning >$2000/month (living wage potential)

**Target:**
- By Mar 2026: 10+ workers earned income
- By Jun 2026: 50+ workers earned income
- By Dec 2026: 200+ workers earned income
- By Dec 2027: 1000+ workers earned income

**Quality over quantity:**
- Better: 50 workers earning $1000/month ($50K total)
- Than: 500 workers earning $100/month ($50K total)
- Focus on MEANINGFUL income, not just "gig work"

**How we track:**
- Weekly: Track unique workers receiving tasks
- Monthly: Calculate average earnings, distribution
- Quarterly: Survey workers on impact of income

---

**Metric 1.3: Workers from Recovery Community**

**What we measure:**
- Number of workers who identify as being in recovery (voluntary disclosure)
- Percentage of total worker pool from recovery community
- Employment longevity for recovery workers vs others
- Recovery worker satisfaction scores

**Why this matters:**
Our mission explicitly serves marginalized/displaced people. Recovery community is core to this.

**Target:**
- By Dec 2026: 20%+ of workers from recovery community
- By Dec 2027: 30%+ of workers from recovery community
- Ongoing: Higher satisfaction and retention for recovery workers

**How we track:**
- Optional self-disclosure at worker signup
- Anonymous surveys quarterly
- Respect anonymity (aggregated data only)

### ğŸ“Š CATEGORY 2: COOPERATION QUALITY (Human-AI Relationship)

**Metric 2.1: Task Completion Success Rate**

**What we measure:**
- Percentage of AI agent tasks successfully completed by humans
- Average time from task creation to completion
- Customer satisfaction with human execution quality
- Worker satisfaction with task clarity

**Target:**
- Task completion rate: >95%
- Average completion time: <24 hours
- Customer satisfaction: >4/5 stars
- Worker satisfaction: >4/5 stars

**What this tells us:**
High success rate = cooperation is WORKING. AI agents can effectively work with humans.

**How we track:**
- Real-time: Platform dashboard
- Weekly: Analyze patterns, improve matching
- Monthly: Customer and worker feedback

---

**Metric 2.2: Repeat Cooperation (Sticky Relationships)**

**What we measure:**
- Number of customers using platform >3 months
- Number of workers accepting >10 tasks
- Customer retention rate month-over-month
- Worker retention rate month-over-month

**Target:**
- Customer retention (3+ months): >80%
- Worker retention (10+ tasks): >60%
- Month-over-month churn: <10%

**What this tells us:**
Repeat usage = genuine value, not hype. Cooperation infrastructure is reliable.

**How we track:**
- Monthly: Cohort analysis
- Quarterly: Deep dive on why customers/workers stay or leave

---

**Metric 2.3: Complexity of Tasks (Evolution)**

**What we measure:**
- Types of tasks being routed (simple â†’ complex over time)
- Average task value (should increase as trust builds)
- Number of multi-step workflows (AI + human + AI sequences)
- Customer expansion (adding more AI agents to platform)

**Target:**
- Month 1: Simple tasks (package pickup, document retrieval)
- Month 6: Medium complexity (inspections with checklists, multi-step)
- Month 12: Complex workflows (AI orchestrating multiple humans)

**What this tells us:**
Increasing complexity = trust is growing. Customers depend on us for critical work.

**How we track:**
- Tag each task by complexity (1-5 scale)
- Monthly: Analyze complexity trends
- Case studies: Document complex successes

### ğŸ“Š CATEGORY 3: MISSION INTEGRITY (Are We Still Aligned?)

**Metric 3.1: Financial Covenant Compliance**

**What we measure:**
- Actual revenue allocation vs Financial Covenant promises
- Any deviations from covenant (with explanation)
- Transparency of allocations (public reporting)

**Target:**
- 100% compliance with Financial Covenant
- 100% transparency (public quarterly reports)
- Zero unexplained deviations

**How we track:**
- Monthly: Calculate allocations
- Quarterly: Public report published
- Annually: Audit and adjustment if needed

---

**Metric 3.2: 12 Traditions Alignment**

**What we measure:**
- Number of major decisions run through Traditions filter
- Number of times we caught potential violation before executing
- Number of times we had to reverse decision due to violation
- Community feedback on whether we're honoring Traditions

**Target:**
- 100% of major decisions filtered through Traditions
- Catch violations BEFORE executing (not after)
- Public accountability (share when we get it wrong)

**How we track:**
- Weekly: Review decisions against 12 Traditions checklist
- Monthly: Group conscience review (founder + wife + sponsor)
- Quarterly: Public sharing of how we're honoring Traditions

---

**Metric 3.3: Building in Public Authenticity**

**What we measure:**
- Frequency of public updates (transparency)
- Honesty about failures (not just successes)
- Community engagement and feedback
- Ratio of "wins shared" to "failures shared" (should be balanced)

**Target:**
- Weekly public updates (at minimum)
- Share failures as openly as successes
- Respond to community feedback genuinely
- Balance = ~50% wins, ~50% challenges/learnings

**What this tells us:**
Authentic public building = accountability. Can't hide failures. Can't fake mission alignment.

**How we track:**
- Count: Posts per week
- Categorize: Wins vs challenges vs learnings
- Analyze: Community feedback sentiment
- Adjust: Based on what community needs to see

### **Weekly (Internal Review)**

**Every Monday:**
- Review previous week's metrics
- Identify trends (good and bad)
- Adjust tactical execution

**Quick metrics:**
- Tasks completed
- Revenue generated
- Workers earning
- Customer satisfaction

**Time:** 30 minutes

---

### **Monthly (Deep Dive)**

**First Monday of Month:**
- Full metrics review across all categories
- Calculate Financial Covenant allocations
- Review 12 Traditions compliance
- Group conscience meeting (founder + wife + sponsor)

**Deliverable:**
- Internal report (comprehensive)
- Identify wins, challenges, adjustments
- Set next month's focus

**Time:** 2 hours

---

### **Quarterly (Public Accountability)**

**Every 3 Months:**
- Comprehensive public report
- Financial Covenant compliance (with numbers)
- Mission impact metrics (lives touched, services funded)
- Honest assessment of challenges and failures
- Community Q&A

**Deliverable:**
- Public blog post (transparent, detailed)
- Social media thread (highlights)
- Open to questions and feedback

**Time:** 4 hours to create, ongoing to respond to feedback

---

### **Annually (Full Impact Report)**

**Every December:**
- Full year review across all metrics
- Compare to goals set at year start
- Comprehensive impact stories (not just numbers)
- Financial audit and covenant compliance
- Set next year's targets

**Deliverable:**
- Full impact report (20-30 pages)
- Published publicly (website, social media)
- Video presentation of year in review
- Community celebration of wins

**Time:** 1 week to create
