## §I: Phase-Aware Completion

**Constitution Principle:** Completion meaning depends on phase. NOETIC: "Have I learned enough?" PRAXIC: "Have I shipped enough?"

**Charter Analysis:**

This charter is **NOETIC work** (research, investigation, feedback gathering, learning). Berlin's own research plan explicitly positions it as "open research project with clearly defined public method" — not a finished product.

**But the charter is structured as PRAXIC:**
- 4 discrete phases with deliverables
- Fixed close date (mechanical calendar deadline)
- Assumes completion happens at Day 90
- No provision for "investigate more" if learning is incomplete

**Violation:** The charter treats noetic work (learning-driven) as if it were praxic work (delivery-driven). 

**Symptom:** We're now on Day 91. The charter was supposed to close yesterday. But the constitution says:
> "Completion is per-transaction, not per-plan. A 1.0 on the current transaction's objective is correct even when subsequent transactions remain."

**Question the charter doesn't answer:** Did we learn enough on June 25 to close on July 16? Or is July 16 arbitrary?

**Verdict:** ❌ VIOLATION — Charter confuses noetic (learning) completion with praxic (delivery) completion.

## §III: Turtle Principle

**Constitution Principle:** "Same epistemic rules at every meta-layer." The rules apply to themselves. A charter is itself subject to the charter discipline.

**Charter Analysis:**

The charter was NOT designed via transaction discipline:
- No PREFLIGHT on the charter itself (what are the epistemic goals? what's the work_type?)
- No CHECK gate before the 90 days began (are we ready to run this structure?)
- No POSTFLIGHT to measure what happened (how did we actually perform?)
- No self-audit mechanism (how would we know if the charter itself was working?)

**The charter has an internal audit mechanism** — each phase includes feedback collection. But it never audits WHETHER THE CHARTER ITSELF IS SOUND.

**Violation:** The charter is exempt from the discipline it enforces. That's anti-turtle.

**Symptom:** I just created an "advisor/PM node" to enforce the charter's discipline. But the charter itself was never subjected to that discipline.

**Verdict:** ❌ VIOLATION — Charter operates outside the transaction discipline it mandates.

## §VI: Sustained Multi-Practice Coordination

**Constitution Principle:** When work spans ≥2 practitioners and outlives one session, binding other practitioners requires authorized shared records.

**Charter Analysis:**

The HumanAIOS charter binds:
- Berlin (external advisor) — asked to review and guide
- Night/Z2 (project owner) — asked to implement phases
- Evaluator (oversight) — asked to assess and report

**But there's no SER that FORMALIZES these commitments.** 

The charter is essentially "Berlin says do this, and Carly will watch." That's informal. It's not a durable, bounded, shared record with role tiers and escalation rules.

**Violation:** Multi-practice commitment without formalized shared epistemic record.

**Symptom:** When the charter close date arrived, there was no clear mechanism for "what do we do now?" because there was no SER recording what each practice committed to and by when.

**Verdict:** ❌ VIOLATION — No SER binds the multi-practice commitment.

## Summary: Transaction Discipline Violations

**The charter violates the core transaction model:**

| Element | Constitution Requires | Charter Does |
|---------|----------------------|--------------|
| **PREFLIGHT** | Open transaction, declare goals + vectors | No PREFLIGHT on the charter itself |
| **Work Phase** | Noetic and praxic phases, clearly distinguished | Mixes noetic (learning) work with praxic (delivery) structure |
| **CHECK** | Gate noetic→praxic with grounded readiness | No CHECK gate before 90 days began |
| **POSTFLIGHT** | Close transaction, measure outcomes | No POSTFLIGHT at Day 90 |
| **SER** | Shared records for multi-practice work | No SER from Day 1; I created SER on Day 91 |
| **Practice Ownership** | One practice owns the epistemic trajectory | Authority fragmented (Berlin advises, evaluator observes, Night owns, Autonomy oversees) |
| **Authority Clarity** | Clear decision-maker per scope | No clear decision-maker at charter close |

**Core Problem:** The charter IS a transaction, but it was never TREATED as a transaction. It ran for 90 days without PREFLIGHT, CHECK, POSTFLIGHT, or SER governance.
