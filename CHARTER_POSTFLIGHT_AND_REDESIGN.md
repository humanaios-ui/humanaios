## PART 2: New Governance Design

### The Problem with the Old Charter

The old charter was designed as a **praxic delivery mechanism** (4 phases → deliverables → close date):
- Berlin drafted it as "here's what you need to do in 90 days"
- Assumes noetic research can be scheduled like product delivery
- Treats "completion" as a fixed date, not achievement of understanding
- Fragments authority (no single decision-maker)
- Lacks constitutional discipline (no PREFLIGHT/CHECK/POSTFLIGHT)

**Diagnosis:** Wrong model for the work.

### What HumanAIOS Actually Needs

HumanAIOS is **noetic work** (research, investigation, learning). It needs:

**Constitutional Foundation:**
- ✅ Practice ownership (one practice decides when "done")
- ✅ Measurement discipline (PREFLIGHT/CHECK/POSTFLIGHT per transaction)
- ✅ SER structure (durable shared records with Berlin, empirica, autonomy)
- ✅ Phase-aware completion (learning-driven, not calendar-driven)
- ✅ Clear authority (Admiral makes final assessment, not advisory role)

**Governance Structure:**
- ✅ Multiple research cycles (not one 90-day sprint)
- ✅ Feedback integration as it arrives (not batched at phases)
- ✅ Rolling refinement (hypothesis → gather data → revise → repeat)
- ✅ Explicit decision gates (when to continue, when to narrow, when to close)
