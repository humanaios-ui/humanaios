## üî¥ CRITICAL COMPLIANCE ISSUES IDENTIFIED

### **ISSUE #1: Tense Inconsistency (MEDIUM RISK)**

**Problem:** Product descriptions use present tense for capabilities that don't exist yet

**Examples:**
- "connects" vs "will connect"
- "handles" vs "will handle"
- "Integration is" vs "Integration will be"

**Legal Risk:**
- Could constitute misrepresentation if customer relies on these statements
- If customer signs contract believing system is operational, we have liability
- FTC false advertising concerns (unlikely to be enforced at our stage, but possible)

**Solution:**
- Revise all product descriptions to future tense OR
- Add clear "Pre-launch" disclaimer at top
- For customer conversations, explicitly state "seeking pilot partners to build this with"

---

### **ISSUE #2: No Clear Staging Disclaimers (LOW-MEDIUM RISK)**

**Problem:** Nowhere in public materials do we explicitly say "pre-launch" or "under development"

**Why it matters:**
- Reasonable person could assume we're operational
- Creates expectation mismatch
- Reduces credibility when they learn we're pre-launch

**Solution:**
- Add to all public materials: "Status: Pre-launch - Seeking Pilot Partners"
- In customer conversations: Lead with development stage
- On website (when built): Clear "Coming Soon" or "Beta Program" language

---

### **ISSUE #3: Partnership/Customer Language Ambiguity (LOW RISK)**

**Problem:** When we say "contacted 5 Fortune 500 companies," it's accurate but could imply deals in progress

**Why it matters:**
- If someone asks "do you have customers?" we need to be clear: "We have outreach conversations, no signed contracts yet"

**Solution:**
- Internal language: "contacted" or "in conversation with"
- External language: "seeking pilot partners at..."
- Never imply deals exist that don't
